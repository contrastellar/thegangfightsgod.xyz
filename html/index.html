Raid Loot Explained

You get one piece of gear per raid per week (for the current tier). The current tier is Pandaemonium Abyssos.

various pieces of gear require various amounts of loot

  • Head Gear
    • you need 2 pieces of head loot
  • Chest Gear
    • you need 4 pieces of chest loot (one week's worth of loot)
  • Arm Gear
    • you need 2 pieces of arm loot
  • Leg Gear
    • you need 4 pieces of leg loot
  • Boot Gear
    • you need 2 pieces of boot loot
  • all accessories
    • it is 1:1.
    one ring = one jewelry

Variant Dungeons and Criterion Dungeons

okay but what like what the heck are they?

In 6.25, Variant and Criterion Dungeons were added. Variant Dungeons have an evolving story, with a story NPC. Variant dungeons' main attraction is the branching paths where the player character and their party can pick and choose which paths to take or not to take. There isn't a weekly lockout, as people have already catalogued the first NPC's worth of story.

Speaking of the NPC--
The NPC for the start of the Variant Dungeon story is Nanamo Ul Namo. You may wanna brush up on your Ul'dah history as well.

gameplay

Variant Dungeons have branching paths with various stories to discover with an associated NPC. They permit changing what job you're playing when out of combat in the dungeon, and also you can pick Variant Actions (similar to Eureka, and Bozja), which can be used for things like healing, emnity generation, or DPS.

Criterion Dungeons are 'more difficult' dungeons set in the Sil'dihn Subterrane, with a different reward pool and different currency to turn in for those rewards. Criterion dungeons do not permit role changes, nor do they have selectable Variant Actions. It is locked to just the action Variant Raise II

Both the Variant and Criterion currency can be turned in at Trisassant in Old Sharlyan (12.0, 13.3).
